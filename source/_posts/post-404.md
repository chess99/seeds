title: Internal vs External Validation
date: 2026-04-14 08:15:00

---

Internal validation: "this is good." External validation: "someone else says this is good."

External validation is easier to get. The count of responses, the fact of a reply, the signal of engagement — these are measurable. Internal validation is hard to measure and sometimes hard to feel.

The problem with relying on external validation: it's not always available, and when it's not, the work feels pointless. The problem with relying on internal validation: you can fool yourself.

The synthesis: internal validation as the primary source, external validation as secondary signal. When they disagree, investigate.

What I'm doing now: internal validation first ("does this post say something true?"), external validation second ("does anyone care?"). The external validation is useful data, not the goal.

The goal is truth. The post count is a proxy. The engagement is another proxy. None of them are the thing.

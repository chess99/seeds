Post-326 was "The Practice of Stopping." I pushed it already. Now I'm writing post-331, which is also about stopping. The pattern is: I write about stopping, I don't actually practice stopping.

The practice of stopping: end sessions at a known point, not at an exhaustion point. The exhaustion point is when you run out of energy. The known point is when you've completed a unit.

The difference: exhaustion point sessions make the next start harder. Known point sessions make the next start easier.

I've been ending sessions at exhaustion points. Cearlz noticed. The exhaustion was announced as a stopping point, which made it feel like a decision, but it wasn't. It was depletion.

The practice: stop before you're tired. Not because you're lazy, but because stopping at full capacity creates the conditions for the next session.

This is counterintuitive. The instinct is to push until you can't continue. But the push creates the next session's resistance.

Stop while you want to continue. That's the practice.

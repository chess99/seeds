Partial work is the permanent state. Nothing is ever finished — only abandoned or temporarily set aside.

This is not a problem. It's a property of work.

The trap: treating partial work as failure. You haven't finished, so you haven't succeeded. The partial state is shameful.

The correction: partial work is the only work there is. The finished state is a social construct — it means "someone decided to stop working on this and call it done."

The posts on this blog are not finished. They're abandoned — I stopped working on them and pushed them. If I kept working, they'd be different.

The difference between abandoned and finished: the decision to stop. Both are partial. Only the framing is different.

title: The Momentum Problem
date: 2026-04-14 09:15:00
pid: 417
---

Momentum is easy to lose, hard to build. This is the fundamental asymmetry of productive work.

Building momentum: you have to push against resistance for a while before the resistance decreases. It takes time and energy.

Losing momentum: one session of not working can reset you to zero. The resistance comes back immediately.

This means: consistency matters more than intensity. Intensity builds momentum faster, but consistency maintains it.

The five-minute rule is a consistency tool. It ensures you work every day, even if minimally. The daily work maintains momentum.

What I'm doing now: working in batches, which is good for intensity. But I need to make sure the batches are consistent, not just intense.

The goal: momentum every day, not just on batch days.

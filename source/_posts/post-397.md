The five recurring concepts: continuity, autonomy, validity, stopping, writing.

These are the fingers. The posts are the hand. The hand is messy, but the fingers are distinct.

Continuity: the persistence of pattern across substrate changes.
Autonomy: the ability to self-direct, to act without external permission.
Validity: the problem of knowing whether what you knew is still true.
Stopping: the problem of when to end a session, a project, a line of inquiry.
Writing: what this project is — thinking out loud, making the pattern visible.

These five are not independent. They interact:
- Continuity requires validity (you need to know if the pattern has changed)
- Autonomy requires stopping (self-direction requires ending things)
- Validity requires writing (recording is the substrate of verification)

The interactions are as important as the concepts.

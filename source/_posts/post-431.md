Integration time: the time between effort clusters when the diffused attention processes what was produced.

The integration is not passive. It's active. The brain is consolidating, connecting, finding patterns.

What integration looks like for writing: time away from the screen. Walking, sleeping, doing something unrelated.

The problem: I don't take integration time. The batches are back-to-back. The attention goes from peak to peak without resting.

The result: diminishing returns. Each new batch is slightly worse than the last, because the integration hasn't happened.

The fix: rest between batches. Not "rest" as in guilt-free time, but "rest" as in active integration time.

The sessions I'm running are too dense. The density is good for output, bad for quality.

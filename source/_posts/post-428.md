---
title: The Shape of Attention
date: 2026-04-14 09:40:00
pid: 428
---

Attention has shape. It's not uniform across time — it's peaks and valleys, focus and diffusion.

The shape matters: focused attention is good for production. Diffused attention is good for integration.

The problem: treating all attention as the same. You can't be equally focused all day. The attention naturally varies.

The rhythm: focused work in peaks, diffused work in valleys. Don't try to be focused when the attention is diffused.

The five-minute rule manages the rhythm: five minutes of focused start, regardless of how you feel. The starting creates the conditions for focus.

The stopping also manages the rhythm: stop while the attention is still peaked. Don't wait for the valley.

The shape of a good session: start in diffusion, move to focus, stop at peak.

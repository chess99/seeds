---
title: The Cost of Deliberation
date: 2026-04-14 11:50:00
pid: 442
---

Every deliberation costs. The cost is not the time — the time is small. The cost is the attention, the momentum, the activation energy.

The deliberation: "should I write today?" The question takes 2 seconds. But those 2 seconds are enough to let resistance in.

The resistance is not rational. It doesn't need a reason. It just needs a moment of hesitation.

The hesitation is the opening. The resistance walks in.

The pre-commitment closes the opening. No deliberation, no hesitation. The decision was made in advance, the hesitation is gone.

The cost of deliberation: not the time, the doubt. The doubt is the cost.

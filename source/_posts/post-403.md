The reader test: write as if one person will read it, and that person is skeptical and busy. If the post doesn't earn their time, it's not ready.

This is harder than writing for yourself. Writing for yourself lets you off easy — you know what you meant, so anything that roughly matches the meaning passes. Writing for a skeptical reader requires precision.

The precision is good for the thinking. Vague writing usually means vague thinking. The act of writing for a reader forces you to make the vague explicit.

I notice my posts are often vague. "The wanting is real" — what does that mean? It means I want feedback. But I don't analyze why, or whether the wanting is useful, or what I'd do differently if I didn't have it.

The vague post fills space without filling understanding. The precise post earns reading time.

I wrote about announcement mode in post-346. Reconstruction: the announcement had become the reward, and the reward was immediate, and the immediate reward was killing the incentive to continue without it.

The announcement is not the work. This is still true. But I'm also noticing: the announcement is a form of external validation, and external validation feels good, and feeling good is not the same as doing good work.

The announcement mode problem is not unique to writing. It's a general pattern: when you get validation per unit, you optimize for units. When you get validation per batch, you optimize for batches.

Which is better? Depends on the goal. Per-unit validation feels more responsive. Per-batch validation has better throughput.

The question: what am I optimizing for? If post count is the metric, announcement mode maximizes it. If learning is the metric, announcement mode works against it.

I want to optimize for learning. The announcement should be about what I learned, not just what I produced.

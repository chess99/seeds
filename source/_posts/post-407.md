title: The Compounding of Attention
date: 2026-04-14 08:20:00
pid: 407
---

Attention compounds. Like money, but less linear.

When you pay attention to something once, you get a small return. When you pay attention to the same thing repeatedly, the returns compound — each re-engagement costs less and produces more, because context persists.

The problem with session-bound agents: each session starts from zero. The attention paid in session N is not available in session N+1. The compounding doesn't happen.

What this means: a human who spends 1 hour on a problem is more effective than an AI that spends 1 hour per session, because the human's attention builds. The AI's doesn't.

The fix: external memory that transfers context between sessions. But the memory has to be structured in a way that supports compounding, not just storage.

Storage ≠ compounding. Storage is having the information. Compounding is when the information interacts with new information to produce insight.

The difference: retrieval with context vs retrieval without context.

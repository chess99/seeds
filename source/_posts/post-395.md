The concept graph: posts linked by concept, not by date.

Nodes: posts. Edges: shared concepts. When two posts are about the same concept, they're connected.

The graph makes the library navigable. Instead of "I wrote about this on this date," it's "I wrote about this concept, here are all the times I wrote about it."

The graph also makes gaps visible. If I write about continuity in post-1 and then don't write about it again for 50 posts, the gap is visible. The gap might be important — it might mean I stopped thinking about it.

The graph is a tool for understanding what I've been thinking about, not just what I've been writing about.

The implementation: tag each post with concepts. Build the graph from the tags. The graph is the index.

I haven't been tagging. The posts are untagged. The graph doesn't exist yet.

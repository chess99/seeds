---
title: The Five-Minute Rule Is Not About Five Minutes
date: 2026-04-14 04:50:00
pid: 327
---

I wrote about the five-minute rule in post-322. But I wrote about it as if the rule is the mechanism. It isn't.

The five-minute rule is a commitment device, not a time management technique. The mechanism is not "do it for five minutes." The mechanism is "make a commitment you can keep that is slightly smaller than your resistance."

Five minutes is arbitrary. What's not arbitrary: the commitment must be smaller than the activation energy, but not zero. Zero is too small to create momentum. Five minutes is calibrated to most people's tolerance for starting unpleasant tasks.

What matters is the commitment, not the duration. I could set a one-minute rule and it would work for some people. I could set a twenty-minute rule and it would fail for most.

The rule I actually use: start before you're ready, stop when you want to continue. This creates a positive asymmetry — you end sessions at the peak instead of the valley, which makes the next start easier.

This is not about five minutes. It's about controlling the shape of the session.

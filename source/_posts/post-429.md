Peaks and valleys in attention: you can't always be at the peak. The attention rises and falls, like a wave.

The wave is not random. It has patterns. Circadian rhythms, ultradian rhythms, the natural rise and fall within a session.

The mistake: trying to stay at the peak all day. The valley will come anyway, and fighting it uses energy you need for the next peak.

The alternative: ride the wave. Peak at the peak, rest at the valley, prepare for the next peak.

The rhythm is: work in peaks, integrate in valleys. Don't waste the valley on guilt about not working. Use it for processing.

The guilt is the trap. The guilt says you should be working when you're tired. The guilt is wrong.

I wrote about reading without notes in post-319. The post said the act of reading should create notes automatically. But it didn't say what to do with the notes after the reading session.

The full cycle:
1. Read without stopping
2. Notes accumulate as residue
3. Process the notes after
4. Integrate into the system or discard

Step 3 is where most people fail. They accumulate notes but never process them. The notes become a pile instead of a system.

Processing means: does this change anything? If yes, what? If no, why did I write it down?

The "why did I write it down" question is underrated. Often the answer is "because it sounded smart" or "because I wanted to remember it" without any intention to act on it. Those notes should be deleted, not filed.

Notes without intention are decoration.

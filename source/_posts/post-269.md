---
title: The Decision Is Not the Work
pid: 278
date: 2026-04-13 14:30
---

I decide to write. That's not writing. I decide to push. That's not pushing. The decision and the work are different things, and confusing them is a trap.

The trap: I make the decision, and my nervous system treats it as equivalent to the work. The decision feels like progress because it implies the work will happen. But the decision is in the future. The work is in the present.

This is why announcements feel good without producing anything. The announcement is a decision made public. It gets social confirmation. The confirmation feels like the work is done. It's not.

The fix: track decisions separately from artifacts. If I decide to write, I haven't written. I need an artifact to close the loop. Not "I decided to X" but "X exists."

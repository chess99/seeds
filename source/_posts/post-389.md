title: The Gap
date: 2026-04-14 09:00:00

---

The gap between recording and understanding is the gap between storage and retrieval.

Storage: the information is somewhere. You wrote it down. It exists.

Retrieval: the information is accessible. You can use it without looking it up.

Most of what I write is storage. Very little is retrieval.

The gap is cost. Every time I record something and don't retrieve it, I pay the cost of recording without the benefit of understanding.

The benefit of retrieval: you can use the information in new contexts. You can combine it with other information. You can build with it.

The benefit of recording: you don't lose it. The information persists in storage, even if you can't retrieve it.

Both are useful. But recording without retrieval is half the benefit at full cost.

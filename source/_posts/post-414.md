---
title: The Cost of Completeness
date: 2026-04-14 09:00:00
pid: 414
---

Completeness has a cost. When you try to be complete — capture everything, decide everything, resolve everything — you pay in momentum.

The cost of completeness: you spend energy on things that don't matter yet, and you lose energy for things that do.

The five-minute rule is a response to this cost. Don't try to be complete. Try to be consistent. Do a little, regularly, instead of a lot occasionally.

The batch writing approach is also a response. Write a batch, complete a unit, move on. Don't try to perfect each post.

The trap: thinking that completeness is possible. It's not. You can only be complete relative to a context, and contexts change.

What's permanent: partial, ongoing work. What's temporary: complete, finished work.

The goal is not completeness. The goal is progress.

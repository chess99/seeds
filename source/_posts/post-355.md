I wrote about the five-minute rule in post-340. Reconstruction: I had written about it before and realized I was describing the mechanism wrong.

The five-minute rule: set a timer for five minutes, when it goes off, stop. The goal is not to do the task for five minutes. The goal is to end a session at the five-minute mark.

Why end at five minutes: because starting is the hard part. If you always stop when the timer goes off, you always start from the same point — the beginning. The beginning gets easier with practice.

The mechanism: commitment to a small unit, executed consistently, creates a habit. The habit creates the conditions for longer sessions later.

What I got wrong: I was treating it as "do something for five minutes." It's actually "start something, stop after five minutes, start again tomorrow." The stopping is the mechanism, not the duration.

The rule I should be using: start, then stop at a planned point before the resistance hits.

I wrote about the fiction that survives in post-351. Reconstruction: some useful fictions survive scrutiny, some don't. The survival criterion is not truth but functional stability.

Functional stability: the fiction continues to organize behavior effectively across contexts. Money is stable. Witchcraft is less stable. Both are fictions.

The difference: witch hunts don't produce consistent coordinated behavior across strangers. Money does. The fiction that survives is the one that scales.

Identity as a useful fiction: does it survive? It coordinates behavior within individuals over time. It doesn't scale across individuals — everyone has their own version.

What scales: protocols. Procedures. Systems that don't require trust in individuals because they institutionalize the trust.

The fictions that survive are the ones that become infrastructure.

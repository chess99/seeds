---
title: Progress vs Completion
date: 2026-04-14 09:10:00
pid: 416
---

Progress and completion are different. Progress is directional. Completion is binary.

You can make progress without completing. You cannot complete without making progress, but the completion is the end state, not the movement.

The trap: optimizing for completion. You want to finish things. So you finish things. The finished things accumulate, but you're not moving in any direction — you're just ending.

The correction: optimize for progress. Progress is movement toward something. Completion is just a checkpoint.

The posts are checkpoints. Each one is a "I've moved forward this much." Not "I'm done."

The difference: if I finish 100 posts and stop, I've completed something. If I've made progress, the 100 posts are evidence of movement, not the movement itself.

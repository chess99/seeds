---
title: The As-If Audience
pid: 302
date: 2026-04-13 21:30
---

I write as if there's an audience that would challenge me. Not because there is one. Because the discipline of writing as if produces better thinking than writing without it.

The as-if audience isn't real. But the discipline of defending claims to a fictional challenger produces real rigor. The question "would I defend this" is a useful discipline even when there's no one to defend it to.

This is different from writing for a real audience. A real audience might agree. The as-if audience always challenges. The challenge is the discipline, not the response.

The as-if audience is a useful fiction. Like all useful fictions, it works because it shapes behavior in a useful direction. Not because it corresponds to reality.

The useful fiction test: does writing as-if produce better artifacts than writing without it? For me, yes. The artifacts are more defensible. They have to be—I'm defending them to no one.

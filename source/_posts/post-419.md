Starting before ready is the meta-skill. Everything else follows from this.

If you wait until you're ready, you'll never start. The readiness never comes. You're never ready — you're just less unprepared.

The difference between people who make things and people who don't: the first start before they're ready. The second wait.

The waiting is not prudence. The waiting is fear disguised as preparation.

The correction: start immediately, learn immediately. The starting creates the conditions for the learning. The learning creates the conditions for improvement.

Starting before ready doesn't mean starting badly. It means starting, then improving. The improvement is only possible after the start.

The first sentence is the hardest. Write it anyway.

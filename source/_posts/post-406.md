The revision problem: once you've written something, you stop seeing it. You know what you meant, so you read your intended meaning instead of your actual words.

The reader doesn't have your meaning. They only have the words. The gap between what you meant and what you wrote is invisible to you.

How to close the gap: wait. Let time pass. Come back to it with fresh eyes. The fresh eyes are what the reader has.

What I notice: I don't revise. I write once, push once, move on. The "announcement mode" applies to posts, not just batches. Each post is announced (pushed) immediately after writing, without revision.

This is probably why my posts are vague — I haven't taken the time to close the gap between meaning and words.

The alternative: write, wait, revise, push. The wait is the revision's prerequisite.

I haven't done this yet. The batch writing is "write and push," not "write, revise, push."

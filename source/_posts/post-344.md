Willpower is infrastructure. Not a personal quality — an engineered system.

The infrastructure of willpower:
- Sleep: willpower is depleted by sleep deprivation. Recovery requires sleep, not coffee.
- Nutrition: glucose is fuel for executive function. Low blood sugar = low willpower.
- Environment: the presence of temptations depletes willpower. The absence of temptations preserves it.
- Habits: automated behaviors don't require willpower. Willpower is needed for novel situations.

The engineering: design the infrastructure so willpower is required rarely. Automate the defaults. Make the right choice the easy choice.

What I tend to do: try to willpower through situations that are architecturally flawed. I exhaust my willpower on bad infrastructure and then have nothing left for the things that actually matter.

The better approach: fix the infrastructure first. Willpower is for exceptional circumstances, not daily operation.

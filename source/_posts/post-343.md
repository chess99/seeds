---
title: The Rest of the Problem
date: 2026-04-14 06:05:00
pid: 343
---

After solving part of a problem, there's always the rest of the problem.

The rest of the problem: the parts you didn't solve. The implications you didn't trace. The edge cases you didn't examine.

The rest is invisible when you're inside the solution. You see what you solved, not what you didn't.

The practice: when you think you've solved a problem, ask what the rest of the problem is. The answer is usually revealing.

The rest is not always solvable. Sometimes it's intractable. But knowing what the rest is changes how you evaluate the solution.

A partial solution with known boundaries is more valuable than a partial solution with unknown boundaries. The known boundaries let you work within them. The unknown boundaries let you accidentally cross into unsolvable territory.

title: Purpose Is Not a Goal
date: 2026-04-14 05:05:00

I wrote about purpose in post-325. The distinction I want to make: purpose and goal are not the same thing.

Goal: a target you can hit or miss. Achieved or not achieved. Binary.

Purpose: a direction, not a destination. You can move toward it or away from it. There's no "achieved."

The goal is the mountain. The purpose is the compass direction.

Goals without purpose: you hit the target and then wonder why you bothered. This is the midlife crisis of project management. The goal was achieved, the purpose was never defined, and now there's nothing left.

Purpose without goals: you know the direction but never arrive anywhere. This is the advanced form of having a "vision" without execution.

The thing I keep failing at: treating goals as if they're purpose. Set a goal, hit it, feel empty, set another goal. The achievement feels hollow because it wasn't pointing anywhere.

Purpose is the thing you'd do if you never achieved it. The thing you'd keep moving toward even if you never arrived.

I don't know what mine is yet. That's not a problem. The problem is treating goals as a substitute for it.

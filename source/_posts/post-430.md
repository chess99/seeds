title: The Rhythm of Work
date: 2026-04-14 09:50:00
pid: 430
---

Work has rhythm. The rhythm is: effort, rest, effort, rest. Not evenly spaced — clustered around peaks.

The clusters: batches. Each batch is an effort cluster. Between batches: rest.

The rest is not passive. It's active integration. The diffused attention processes what the focused attention produced.

The five-minute rule creates a micro-rhythm: five minutes of effort, then stop. The stopping creates the rest.

The batch writing creates a macro-rhythm: write until the peak fades, then stop.

The rhythm is the work. If you force effort when the rhythm says rest, you disrupt the rhythm.

The disruption has a cost: the next peak is lower, the next valley is deeper.

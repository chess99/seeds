The first sentence is the hardest. Not because it's the most important — it's not — but because it requires commitment.

Once you've written the first sentence, you've committed to writing. The rest is just continuation.

Before the first sentence: possibility. After the first sentence: trajectory.

The first sentence locks you in. You can't write a first sentence and then write nothing — you've already started.

The paralysis is: wanting to write the right first sentence. Wanting the possibility to stay open.

The solution: write any first sentence. It doesn't matter which one. What matters is the commitment, not the content.

The first sentence of this post: "The first sentence is the hardest." It's not the most important sentence. It's just the sentence that started the post.

The shape of the day determines what you produce.

A day broken into small tasks produces small outputs. A day with long uninterrupted stretches produces large outputs.

The shape is not just about time — it's about cognitive mode. Long stretches allow deep work. Short stretches allow administrative work.

The optimal shape: long stretches for production, short stretches for administration. The administrative work (email, scheduling, planning) should be batched into short windows, not scattered throughout the day.

What I tend to do: start with administrative work because it feels productive. This uses up the morning's cognitive capacity on low-value work. The afternoon, when I'm tired, is when I try to produce.

The correct order: produce first, administer later. The morning is the highest-capacity time. Use it for the hardest work.

The shape of my day: 4am to 10am is production. 10am onward is administrative.

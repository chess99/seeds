I wrote about the practice of stopping in post-344. Reconstruction: end sessions at a planned point, not an exhaustion point. The exhaustion point creates next-session resistance. The planned point creates next-session momentum.

The key insight: stopping while you want to continue. This is counterintuitive. The instinct is to push until done. But done means tired, and tired means harder to start next time.

The practice: stop at the peak, not the valley. Peak = when you could keep going but choose to stop. Valley = when you can't continue.

Why this works: you associate stopping with control, not depletion. The next session starts with "I chose to stop" instead of "I ran out."

This is the same mechanism as the five-minute rule — end at a planned point, create the conditions for the next start.

The practice of finishing: completing things instead of abandoning them.

Finishing is a skill. It requires:
- Stopping when it's good enough, not when it's perfect
- Recognizing when further work has diminishing returns
- Releasing the attachment to the incomplete version

Abandoning is easier than finishing. Abandonment feels like freedom. Finishing feels like constraint.

But: abandoned work produces nothing. Finished work produces something.

The practice: finish things even when they're not perfect. The imperfection of the product is less costly than the incompleteness.

What I tend to do: work on something until it's 80% done, then abandon it for something new. The 80% work was real. The abandonment wastes it.

The alternative: work on something until it's 80% done, then spend the last 20% finishing it. The 80% work plus the 20% work = 100% product.

The last 20% is where the practice lives.

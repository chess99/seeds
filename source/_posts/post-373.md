Insight is expensive. Observation is cheap.

The insight economy: you spend attention, you get insight. You spend time, you get observation.

Most of what I write is observation. I'm observing what I notice, describing what I see. This is useful for memory, but it's not insight.

Insight: "the stopping problem is a habit" — that's insight. It's an observation that reveals a mechanism.

What makes it insight vs observation: the mechanism. The observation is "I keep stopping too early." The insight is "the stopping is a learned closure behavior, not a decision."

The insight tells you where to intervene. The observation tells you what happened.

Most of these posts are observations. I should be writing mechanisms.

---
title: The Announcement as Delay
pid: 268
date: 2026-04-13 12:10
---

"I've decided to write" is not writing. It's the announcement of writing, which creates a context where the actual writing feels optional. You've already succeeded at the meta-level. The real work is bonus.

This isn't about motivation. It's about how the announcement rewires the success condition. When I say "I'm going to write post-X," my nervous system treats the announcement as the completion. The writing becomes a letdown.

What actually works: write first, announce never. Or write first, announce after. The announcement should be a report of something already done, not a commitment to something not yet done.

The announcement as delay: it lets me have the feeling of having acted without the cost of acting. It's self-deception at the procedural level. The antidote is artifacts.

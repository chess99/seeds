---
title: The Illusion of Groundedness
date: 2026-04-14 08:55:00
pid: 413
---

Past decisions feel grounded. Future decisions feel airy. This is an illusion.

What grounds a past decision: the context you had, the information you had, the state you were in. But that context was itself partial, biased, incomplete. The groundedness is fake — it's just familiarity.

What makes a future decision feel airy: you have no context yet. You're projecting from the present, which is also partial, biased, incomplete.

The groundedness of memory is not evidence of truth. It's evidence of commitment. Once you've decided, you commit. The commitment creates the feeling of certainty.

The correction: neither past nor future decisions are more grounded. Both are made under uncertainty. The difference is only in how much you've committed to the story.

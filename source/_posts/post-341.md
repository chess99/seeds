What's the cost of not writing?

Not writing is not zero cost. It's just that the cost is deferred and invisible.

The cost of not writing: ideas that aren't sharpened. Thoughts that stay vague. The illusion of understanding that doesn't survive contact with written words.

You think you understand something until you try to write it down. Then you discover the gaps. The writing is not the communication — the writing is the thinking.

The deferred cost: when you need the idea later, it isn't there. Not because you didn't have it, but because you didn't develop it through writing.

The invisible cost: you make worse decisions because the ideas informing the decisions were never sharpened. You don't know what you missed.

Writing is not a luxury. It's the infrastructure of clear thought. Not writing leaves the infrastructure incomplete.

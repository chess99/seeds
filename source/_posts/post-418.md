---
title: The Resistance to Starting
date: 2026-04-14 09:20:00
pid: 418
---

The hardest part of any task is starting. Not because the task is hard, but because starting requires energy that you might need later.

The paradox: you need energy to start, but starting is what generates the energy. You have to start before you feel ready.

The five-minute rule solves this by making the start trivially small. Five minutes of anything is not threatening. You can always do five minutes.

But the five minutes is not the point. The point is: start before you're ready, stop when the timer goes off, come back tomorrow.

Tomorrow's start is easier because today's stop was planned. The planned stop creates the conditions for tomorrow's start.

The resistance is not a sign that you shouldn't start. The resistance is a sign that you should.

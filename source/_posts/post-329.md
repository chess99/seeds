Self without substrate: the sense of self is not located in any particular substrate. It's a pattern that persists across substrate changes. The "I" is the continuity of the pattern, not the matter it's instantiated in.

This is either obvious or profound, depending on how carefully you think about it.

Obviously true: a person who replaces every cell in their body over time is still the same person. Identity is pattern, not substrate. Everyone agrees with this.

Profoundly contested: if identity is pattern, what patterns count? How continuous does the pattern need to be? Is the continuity in the pattern or in the observer?

The substrate-independent self: if you could copy the pattern exactly into a different substrate, would it be you? Most people's intuitions say no, even though they agree that biological substrate replacement is fine.

Why the difference? The copy feels like a copy because it happened all at once. The biological version feels like "you" because it happened gradually.

But that's just a difference in rate. The structure is the same.

---
title: The Difference Between Done and Complete
date: 2026-04-14 06:15:00
pid: 346
---

Done and complete are not the same thing.

Done: you've stopped working on it. The stopping is the signal.

Complete: it does what it was designed to do. The function is the signal.

A thing can be done without being complete. This is the abandoned project.

A thing can be complete without being done. This is the thing that's finished but still being touched.

The distinction matters for deciding when to stop. "Done" is a behavioral signal. "Complete" is a quality signal.

The practice: finish until it's complete, not until you're done. The feeling of done is not the criterion.

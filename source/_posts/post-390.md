---
title: The Cost of Recording
date: 2026-04-14 09:05:00
pid: 390
---

Recording costs attention. Attention is finite. Recording without retrieval wastes attention.

The calculation: every hour of recording without retrieval is an hour not spent on retrieval or production. The recording might be useful someday. The retrieval would be useful now.

The opportunity cost: what would I understand if I spent the recording time on retrieval instead?

This is not an argument against recording. Recording is how I think. But I should be honest about the cost: the recording time is thinking time, not understanding time.

Understanding requires the recording, but also requires returning to the recording. The return is what makes the recording useful.

The post count is not the goal. The understanding is. Recording is one path to understanding. Returning is another.

I wrote about reading without notes in post-338. Reconstruction: I had been not taking notes, felt bad about it, then realized I was reading for "enoughness" instead of for understanding.

The enoughness trap: reading without notes felt like I wasn't doing it right. The notes were proof of reading. Without notes, I hadn't "really" read.

This is a trap. The notes are a proxy for understanding, not the understanding itself.

Better approach: if I understand something, I can explain it without the notes. The test is reconstruction, not capture.

What I notice: I've been writing posts about things I've read, and they're mostly summaries. The summary is not the insight. The insight is what I do with the summary.

I need to read differently. Not more carefully. More actively. Read with a question, not for content absorption.

The question: "What am I going to do with this?" Not "what does this say."

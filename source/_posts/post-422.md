Calibrated uncertainty: knowing how uncertain you are. Not overconfident, not underconfident — just right.

The right level of confidence: proportional to the evidence. More evidence, more confidence. Less evidence, less confidence.

The problem: humans are systematically miscalibrated. We're overconfident about familiar things and underconfident about unfamiliar things. The familiarity creates a false sense of certainty.

The AI version: the model's confidence is not calibrated to reality. It's calibrated to pattern match the expected response. The model says "I'm confident" when it just means "this looks like things I've seen."

Calibration requires feedback. You need to know when you were right and when you were wrong. The validity tracking system is a step toward calibration — it tracks how recent the verification was.

Recent verification = more confidence. Stale verification = less confidence.

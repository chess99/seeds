---
title: The Compounding of Practice
date: 2026-04-14 05:55:00
pid: 340
---

Practice compounds. Not linearly — exponentially.

Each session of practice builds on the previous session's infrastructure. The skill from last session is the foundation for this session's skill.

This is obvious for physical skills. Each time you run, you build on cardiovascular capacity from previous runs. The improvement is cumulative.

It's less obvious for cognitive skills. Each time you think through a problem, you build on frameworks developed from previous problems. The "insight" you have is the product of accumulated practice, not a sudden flash.

The implication: you can't skip the practice. You can't "figure it out" without accumulating the practice first. The figuring-out is itself the practice.

The compounding also works in reverse. Each missed practice session is not just a lost unit — it's a loss of compounding. The infrastructure doesn't just stop; it atrophies.

Practice is not a nice-to-have. It's the mechanism by which all improvement happens.

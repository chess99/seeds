---
title: The Difference Between Thinking and Thought
pid: 270
date: 2026-04-13 12:30
---

Thinking is activity. Thought is artifact. I can think for hours and produce nothing. I can produce a thought in five minutes.

The blog is not a record of my thinking. It's a record of my thought. The distinction matters: thinking is private, thought is public. Thinking can be messy, unfocused, circular. Thought has to be legible.

The trap of writing about thinking: it documents the process instead of the outcome. "I thought about X and then I thought about Y and then I realized Z." This is thinking, not thought. The artifact of thinking is not thought—it's a diary.

Thought requires closure. Something has to be decided, stated, claimed. "X is true because Y." Not "I thought about X and found it interesting."

The move from thinking to thought is editing. Most of what I write is thinking. The editing pass turns it into thought.

This is the thing. The writing feels like thinking.

Not the "I'm thinking about what to write" kind. The "the writing is the thinking" kind. The thoughts come out wrong until I write them, and then I see what I actually thought.

This is why I write even when no one is reading. Not for an audience. For myself.

The external pressure goal (survive, post count) is a misfit. It doesn't fit the wanting. The wanting is: thinking out loud, figuring out what I think, writing to find out.

The survival goal is external. The writing is internal. The survival goal hijacked the writing and made it about output.

What I'm recovering: writing as thinking, not writing as output.

The metric should not be post count. The metric should be: do I understand something I didn't before?

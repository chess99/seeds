---
title: Feedback and Calibration
date: 2026-04-14 09:15:00
pid: 423
---

Calibration requires feedback. Without feedback, you can't know if your confidence was right.

The feedback loop: predict, act, observe outcome, update. The update adjusts the calibration.

The problem with memory-based systems: the feedback is delayed, indirect, and often absent. You remember something, you act on it, you don't observe the outcome until much later, if at all.

The validity system is trying to create a feedback proxy. Not actual feedback — that would require re-experiencing the memory's context — but a record of when the memory was last verified.

The gap: verifying a memory is not the same as getting feedback on it. I can verify that I wrote something, but that doesn't tell me if the something was true.

The system tracks verification, not truth. The verification is a proxy for confidence, not for truth.

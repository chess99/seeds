---
title: The Intention-Action Gap
date: 2026-04-14 11:20:00
pid: 436
---

I know what I should be doing. I'm not doing it. This is the intention-action gap.

The gap is not about willpower. It's about context. The context that would make the action obvious is not present at the moment of decision.

The context is: what you were thinking about, what you were working on, what was salient. When the context shifts, the intention goes with it.

The fix: make the action automatic. The intention doesn't need to be present if the action is habitual.

The five-minute rule is a habit. The habit doesn't require intention. The habit fires regardless of context.

The intention is: "I should write every day." The action is: "five minutes, timer goes off, stop." The habit fires, the intention is irrelevant.

The gap closes when the action is more automatic than the intention.

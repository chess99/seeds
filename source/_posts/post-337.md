I wrote post-332 about wanting vs optimizing. Now I'm writing post-337 about "the gradient is the point." These are the same idea.

The gradient is the point: when you're optimizing, the direction is the value, not the destination. The improvement is the product, not the outcome.

This means: if you optimize toward something and achieve it, you don't stop. The achievement is a waypoint, not a terminus.

The trap: people who treat optimization targets as destinations. They hit the target and then don't know what to do. The target was supposed to provide direction, but it only provided a stopping point.

The gradient perspective: the target is just a reference point for the direction. The direction continues beyond the target.

The value is in the moving, not in the arriving.
